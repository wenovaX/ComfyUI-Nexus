[h]What is Nexus?[/h]

Nexus is a dedicated all-in-one tool that automatically manages the installation, execution,
environment setup, and updates of ComfyUI for you. Think of it as a [b]"veteran studio manager"[/b]
dispatched to ensure you can work in a flawless AI environment.

[accent]If ComfyUI is a highly powerful but tricky machine to handle,
Nexus acts as your reliable guide, taking care of all the complicated background work.[/accent]

---

[h]How It Works: "Waking Up the Magic Studio"[/h]

To start your AI artistry, Nexus lights up your studio in the following order.

[b]1. Nexus (The Smart Agent)[/b]

This is the core element that moves first.
Like an [b]"agent"[/b] dispatched to ComfyUI in advance, Nexus organizes the room,
checks the tools, and creates a pleasant environment for you to work in.

[b]2. Python (The Foundation of the Studio)[/b]

ComfyUI fundamentally operates on a programming language called Python.
Nexus automatically configures this Python environment so that you
don't need to understand any complex code.

[b]3. WebView (The Workspace Itself)[/b]

Without any complicated web browser setups or cumbersome extra installations,
users can open the ComfyUI screen just like a regular program and dive right into creating.

[b]4. Git (The Supply Route for Useful Tools)[/b]

This serves as the [b]"official channel"[/b] used to safely bring in not only the core ComfyUI,
but also countless extra features (custom nodes) created by developers worldwide into your studio.

---

[h]What is Needed (And Why?)[/h]

[h]1. Python[/h]

This is the [b]"core engine."[/b] Just like a car needs an engine to move,
Python is absolutely required for ComfyUI to think and generate images.

[h]2. Virtual Environment (venv)[/h]

This is your [b]"dedicated toolbox."[/b] Instead of scattering tools on the floor,
it's like keeping them in an organized box. This prevents your computer from
getting messy and avoids conflicts with other programs.
[img:python_environment.png|800]

[h]3. PyTorch & CUDA[/h]

This is your [b]"hyper-speed power."[/b]

→ [b]PyTorch[/b] is the core tool that AI uses to actually perform calculations.
→ [b]CUDA[/b] borrows the power of your graphics card to vastly improve generation speed.

[accent]Without these, your AI would be as slow as a snail![/accent]

[b]4. Git[/b]

This is the [b]"universal connector."[/b] It is used when downloading the latest
features and new nodes created by creators all over the world.
