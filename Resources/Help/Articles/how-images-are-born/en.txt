[h]How Images are Born: Finding Form in the Clay[/h]

[img:ai_sculptor.png|768]

The process of AI generating an image is closer to sculpting a statue from a rough block of clay, 
rather than drawing a picture on a blank piece of paper.

The reason we wire nodes in ComfyUI is to personally design this [b]'image sculpting process.'[/b]

[accent]This process is known as [b]'Diffusion.'[/b] It's a scientific magic that finds meaningful shapes within meaningless noise.[/accent]

---

[h]The Generation Phases[/h]

[b]1. Prompt and VAE (The Blueprint and the Converter)[/b]

The prompt serves as the [b]'blueprint'[/b] that determines what kind of sculpture to make.
The actual generation happens in the [b]'Latent Space,'[/b] an internal workspace that humans cannot see.
The VAE acts as a converter, traveling between the image we see and this latent space.

[b]2. Checkpoint (The Sculptor's Mind and Art Style)[/b]

The checkpoint model represents the sculptor's [b]'skills and unique art style.'[/b]
Depending on whether the model is realistic or anime, it can look at the same prompt (blueprint) 
and create completely different artworks. You can think of it as the AI's massive knowledge base.

[b]3. Sampler (The Sculptor and Their Tools)[/b]

This is the core phase where the sculptor actually takes the chisel to carve the clay. 
Starting from random noise, it's an iterative process of refining the shape, determining how much care is put in.

→ [b]Steps (Carving Quality):[/b] The number of times the chisel is swung.
  - [b]Lower:[/b] Leaves the image in a rough, incomplete state filled with noise.
  - [b]Higher:[/b] Refines the details, but going beyond a certain point only wastes time.

→ [b]CFG Scale (Adherence to Blueprint):[/b] How strictly the sculptor follows the prompt (blueprint).
  - [b]Lower:[/b] Ignores the prompt to paint freely, but results in a blurry image.
  - [b]Higher:[/b] Obsesses over the prompt, but going too high causes burned colors or distorted shapes.

→ [b]Denoise (Re-creation Strength):[/b] Determines how much of the original image is kept versus redrawn.
  (This is particularly important in img2img or upscale/refining workflows.)
  - [b]Lower:[/b] Preserves the original shape, only lightly touching up fine details.
  - [b]Higher:[/b] Boldly discards the original frame, creating a completely new picture.

[b]4. VAE Decode and Output (Final Polishing and Exhibition)[/b]

The raw data immediately after carving is still in a mathematical state that we cannot see.
Only after passing through the [b]'developing/printing'[/b] process of VAE decoding is it converted 
into clean pixel data visible to our eyes, which is then finally displayed on the screen.

---

[h]Why is the Result Different Every Time?[/h]

It's because the sculptor starts their work on a different block of clay (Seed) every time.
The [b]Seed[/b] is a unique value that determines the initial noise pattern.
Even with the same prompt (blueprint), a different initial noise leads to a completely different texture.

[accent]Generation is rarely completed in a single try.
Change the checkpoint to switch knowledge bases,
adjust the sampler to change carving techniques, and discover the most beautiful shape you dream of.[/accent]
